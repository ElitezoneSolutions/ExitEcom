# **ExitEcom Dashboard Review:** 

## **SECTION 1 — WHAT TO KEEP, CUT & CHANGE (NAVIGATION)**

### **✅ Keep in MVP Sidebar**

* Dashboard  
* Business Profile  
* Data Sources  
* Exit Readiness Score  
* Risk Scanner  
* Valuation Engine  
* Optimization Plan  
* Saved Reports / Downloads  
* Settings / Billing

### **❌ Remove from MVP Sidebar**

* Financial Normalizer  
* Investment Memo  
* Data Room

**Why remove, not just lock?** Because showing locked features in the nav creates two problems: (1) users feel the product is incomplete, and (2) it dilutes focus. For the MVP, you want every navigation item to feel polished and finished. Replace these three with a single upsell CTA in the sidebar: **"Find a Buyer →"** (locked behind premium). This keeps the value promise alive without the clutter.

## **SECTION 2 — SCREEN-BY-SCREEN CRITICAL ANALYSIS**

**DASHBOARD — What's Good, What to Fix, What to Add**

#### **What's Working**

* The three-card hero (Exit Score / Estimated Value / Value Left on the Table) is extremely strong. The blue £80,000 card creates immediate emotional urgency.  
* "Top Buyer Concerns" with pound-value impact (£-45k, £-35k, £-40k) is exactly right,  it speaks to what a buyer will actually think.  
* "Business Snapshot" at the bottom grounds the data in real metrics.  
* The "Highest Impact Actions" panel is a clear conversion bridge to Optimization Plan.

#### **What to Fix**

1. **"Risk-adjusted value is suppressed"** This label under the Exit Score is passive. Change it to something more visceral: *"Buyers are discounting your business right now."*

2. **"Last updated: Today, 09:34"**  Move this to a less prominent position. It's noise in the hero area.

3. **Data Health bar at the bottom**: Currently feels like an afterthought. This is actually one of your best retention hooks. Elevate it to a more prominent card with a clear CTA: *"Your score is based on 65% of your data. Connect P\&L and GA4 to unlock a more accurate valuation."*

4. **"3 additional risks identified"**  This is a crucial conversion trigger that's being wasted as small text. Make it a locked/blurred card: *"3 more risks suppressing your value \[Unlock with Pro\]"*.

5. **The score of 62**. Add context: *"You're in the top 40% of businesses we've analysed"* OR *"The average business we see scores 54, you're above average, but still leaving significant value behind."* Benchmarking creates both pride and urgency.

#### **What to Add**

* **"Find a Verified Buyer" CTA card** — This is your most powerful premium hook on the dashboard. Add a card in the top right area (or below the three hero cards) that reads:

   *"12 buyers are actively looking for businesses like yours."*

   **\[See Matched Buyers — Pro Only\]**

   This number should feel real and dynamic. Even if it's a placeholder, it creates the sense of a live marketplace.

* **"Your Exit Timeline" mini widget** — A simple indicator: *"At your current score, you're 3–6 months from exit-ready. Unlock your plan to accelerate."* This answers the \#1 question sellers have: *how long will this take?*

* **Progress loop prompt** — If the user has been on the platform before, show delta: *"Your score improved 4 points since last week. Keep going."* Retention through progress.

* **"What a buyer sees" toggle** — A small button that says *"Switch to Buyer View"* which blurs/reframes the dashboard as if a buyer were seeing it. This is psychologically powerful and is a premium-only feature.

### **BUSINESS PROFILE — What's Good, What to Fix, What to Add**

#### **What's Working**

* Clean, simple form. Good structure.  
* Store Snapshot sidebar (Shopify connected, Meta connected, Monthly Revenue, Channel) is a nice at-a-glance summary.

#### **What to Fix**

1. **Empty Description field** — This is a missed opportunity. Add placeholder text that guides the user: *"Tell buyers what makes this business unique. What is the brand story? What would a new owner be acquiring?"* This primes them to think like a seller.  
2. **"Last updated: 2 hours ago"** — Good to have, but add a nudge: *"Buyers verify your profile details during due diligence. Keep this up to date."*  
3. **Business Age: 2.4 years** — This is actually a risk flag (limited operating history). The profile should surface this quietly: *"⚠️ Business age below 3 years may compress your multiple. See Risk Scanner."* Connect the data points.

#### **What to Add**

* **Seller Intent field** — *"What is your timeline for exit?"* (Dropdown: 0–6 months / 6–12 months / 12–24 months / Just exploring). This is critical data for buyer matching AND it deepens the seller's commitment to the process.  
* **Asking Price input** — *"Do you have a target price in mind?"* Many sellers come in anchored to a number. Capturing this lets you show them the gap: *"Your target is £350k. Current fair market value is £220k. Here's the gap and how to close it."* This is an incredibly powerful conversion moment.  
* **"Seller Strengths" free text** — *"What are the 3 strongest things about this business?"* This feeds into the Investment Memo later and makes the seller feel heard and engaged.

### **DATA SOURCES — What's Good, What to Fix, What to Add**

#### **What's Working**

* Data Confidence score (72) is a great engagement hook.  
* The "Connect P\&L and GA4 to reach 90%+" instruction is clear and actionable.  
* Category grouping (Store / Marketing / Financial / Analytics) is clean.

#### **What to Fix**

1. **"NOT CONNECTED" labels** — These are neutral. They should be urgency-driving. Change to: *"Missing — affects your score"* in amber/orange. This creates a clear cost to inaction.  
2. **TikTok Ads / Snapchat Ads** — For an MVP, these platforms are noise for most UK e-commerce businesses. Consider hiding them behind a "More Platforms" expand option to reduce visual clutter and make the critical connections (P\&L, GA4) stand out more.  
3. **No explanation of WHY each data source matters** — Add a one-liner under each: *"P\&L Upload — Without this, your valuation range is £40k wider than it needs to be."* Make the cost of not connecting visceral.

#### **What to Add**

* **Data source impact calculator** — When a user connects a new source, show a micro-animation: *"Data Confidence: 72% → 81% — Your valuation range narrowed by £20k."* Instant reward for action.  
* **"Coming soon" sources** — Bank statements, Amazon Seller Central, WooCommerce. Showing these as "Coming soon" signals ambition and gives advanced users a reason to stay.  
* **Privacy/security badge** — Business owners are paranoid about sharing financial data. Add a visible note: *"Bank-grade encryption. Your data is never shared with buyers without your permission."* This is a conversion blocker you're not addressing right now.

### **EXIT READINESS SCORE — What's Good, What to Fix, What to Add**

#### **What's Working**

* 9-dimension breakdown is genuinely impressive and differentiating. This is not something competitors do.  
* The radar chart visual (polygon shape) is distinctive.  
* Three-scenario valuation (Quick Sale / Fair Market / Optimised) is excellent framing.  
* The "You are leaving approximately £80,000 in potential exit value unrealised" callout with the specific drivers (Founder Dependency £-35k, Product Concentration £-45k) is the aha moment. This is your best screen.

#### **What to Fix**

1. **The radar chart is too small and abstract** — Most users won't understand what they're looking at. Either make it larger with labelled axes, or replace it with a more legible visual like a spider/hex chart with the dimension names visible.

2. **Score description: "Strong growth trajectory with suppressed buyer confidence due to operational concentration risks and founder dependency"** — This is good copy but it's passive. Rewrite as: *"Your growth is real — but buyers will still discount you by 0.5x because of two fixable problems. Here's exactly what they see."* Make it feel like inside information.

3. **Dimension scores without context** — Showing "Founder Dependency: 3/10" is alarming but not actionable without context. Add a one-line explainer on each: *"3/10 — Critical. Buyers see this as a job, not an asset."*

4. **Data Confidence 72%** — Add a note: *"Your score may improve when you connect P\&L and GA4. \[Add Data Sources\]"* Keep driving back to data completeness.

#### **What to Add (Free vs Premium Gating)**

This is the most important screen for your freemium conversion. Here's what to show free vs. gate:

**FREE (show fully):**

* Overall score: 62/100  
* Top 3 dimension scores  
* Valuation range: £180k–£260k  
* The £80k value gap statement

**GATE behind Pro (blur/lock):**

* Dimensions 4–9 (show as blurred rows with a lock icon)  
* The full radar chart (show as blurred)  
* The individual dimension narrative (what each score means and why)  
* The "Optimised" scenario valuation (£340k) — show the number but blur the methodology  
* The "Ready to unlock your value gap?" section should be more urgent: *"2 risks are costing you £80,000. Your competitors are already preparing their exits. Unlock the full plan."*

**Premium upsell copy on this page:**

*"You've seen your score. Now see exactly what a buyer sees — and what you can do about it."*

 **\[Unlock Full Analysis — £499/mo\]**

### **RISK SCANNER — What's Good, What to Fix, What to Add**

#### **What's Working**

* Risk Score (48/100) with label ("Moderate Risk") is clear.  
* £120k estimated value lost is a powerful anchor.  
* "6 total — 2 High · 3 Medium · 1 Low" summary is well structured.  
* "View Buyer Psychology" expands is a brilliant feature — this is genuinely unique and should be highlighted more.  
* The Current → Potential value bar (£220k → £340k) at the bottom is strong.

#### **What to Fix**

1. **"Buyer Psychology" is buried** — This is your most differentiated feature. The fact that you show sellers *why buyers psychologically react to these risks* is incredibly powerful. Promote this more: rename it *"Why Buyers Walk Away"* and make it more prominent.

2. **The risk cards are uniform visually** — High risks should feel alarming (red border, warning icon), medium risks should feel urgent (amber), low risks should feel manageable (green). The current colour dots are too subtle.

3. **"3 Additional Risks" accordion** — This is your premium gate on this screen. Change it to: *"3 more risks identified — costing you an estimated £XX,000 \[Unlock with Pro\]"* with a blurred preview of the risk titles.

#### **What to Add**

* **"What a buyer would ask you in due diligence"** — Under each risk, show 2–3 questions a buyer would ask about this risk. Example for Product Concentration: *"A buyer will ask: What happens to revenue if this SKU is discontinued? Do you have supplier exclusivity? What's the development pipeline?"* This is incredibly practical and makes the risk feel real.  
* **Risk trend indicator** — *"This risk has been flagged for 3 weeks. The longer it persists, the harder it is to fix before exit."* Creates urgency through time pressure.  
* **Peer benchmarking** — *"72% product concentration is in the highest-risk quartile for businesses your size. The median for comparable businesses is 45%."* Social proof through benchmarks.  
* **"Fix This Risk" quick action** — Each risk card should have a *"See Fix →"* button that deep links to the relevant Optimization Plan action. Reduce friction between insight and action.

#### **Free vs. Premium Gating**

**FREE:** Top 2–3 risks (titles only, no detail)

 **GATE:** Risk descriptions, Buyer Psychology, valuation impact per risk, the fix actions, all additional risks

### **VALUATION ENGINE — What's Good, What to Fix, What to Add**

#### **What's Working**

* "What a buyer will actually pay — and why" is excellent positioning. It differentiates from Flippa/Acquire.com estimates.  
* Positive/Negative Drivers split view is clean and educational.  
* Three scenarios (Quick Sale / Fair Market / Optimised) give real context.  
* "You're leaving £80,000 on the table" in blue on the right is the key emotional hook.

#### **What to Fix**

1. **The valuation range inconsistency** — The dashboard shows £180k–£260k, the Valuation Engine shows £180k–£260k at the top but the three scenarios show £90k / £160k / £240k. These don't align. Quick Sale should not be £90k if the range starts at £180k. This will immediately erode trust with sophisticated users. Reconcile these numbers.  
2. **"Adjusted Earnings: £100,000"** — This is shown without explanation. Add: *"Based on your reported EBITDA of £84k, adjusted for owner salary add-backs and one-off costs."* Even if approximate, showing the methodology builds trust.  
3. **"Your Value Opportunity" progress bar** — The bar showing £220k current vs £300k potential is too passive. Make it interactive: *"Move the slider to see how each action increases your valuation."*  
4. **Negative Drivers each showing "-0.3x"** — This is meaningful but abstract. Translate it: *"Founder Dependency is reducing your multiple by 0.3x — that's £66,000 at your current earnings level."*

#### **What to Add**

* **"How we calculate this" accordion** — Business owners are sceptical of AI valuations. Add a transparent methodology section: *"We analyse 9 buyer-critical dimensions, cross-reference against 200+ e-commerce transactions, and apply risk-adjusted multiples based on real buyer behaviour."* This builds the credibility that justifies the subscription.  
* **Multiple sensitivity table** — *"At 2x multiple: £200k. At 2.4x: £240k. At 3x: £300k. Here's what drives multiples above 2.5x."* Interactive and educational.  
* **"Get a Second Opinion" premium feature** — *"Book a 30-minute valuation review with an exit advisor. They'll validate your number and tell you what buyers in our network would actually pay."* This is a high-value premium add-on that bridges the gap between software and human advice.  
* **Comparable exits** — *"Businesses like yours have recently sold for £180k–£320k. Here are 3 anonymised comparables."* (Premium gated). Real comps are what brokers charge for. You can provide this.

#### **Free vs. Premium Gating**

**FREE:** Valuation range, Fair Market value, top 2 positive/negative drivers

**GATE:** Full positive/negative driver analysis with £ translation, the Optimised scenario methodology, comparable exits, valuation methodology detail, advisor consultation booking

### **OPTIMIZATION PLAN — What's Good, What to Fix, What to Add**

#### **What's Working**

* "£120,000 Total Unlock Potential" is a powerful anchor.  
* "8 total — 3 High · 3 Medium · 2 Quick Wins" framing is well structured.  
* "2–3 months" estimated time is realistic and reassuring.  
* The Implementation Roadmap (Quick Wins / Short-Term / Strategic) is excellent — it reduces overwhelm by breaking down a big problem.  
* Progression Summary (£220k → £255k → £340k) is motivating.

#### **What to Fix**

1. **Action cards are too uniform** — High Priority cards need to feel more urgent. Add a *"Buyers will discount you for this today"* sub-label on HIGH priority items.  
2. **"View Action Steps ∨"** — When expanded, this needs to be genuinely actionable and specific. Not *"Reduce product concentration"* but *"Step 1: Identify your top 3 highest-margin SKUs. Step 2: Set a revenue target for SKU \#2 within 90 days. Step 3: Add 1 complementary product this month."* If the action steps are too vague, users won't act and won't stay subscribed.  
3. **The roadmap checkboxes (Quick Wins)** — These should be interactive and persistent. If a user checks *"Add trust badges to PDP"*, that should register progress and update their score. This is your retention loop.  
4. **"Download Full Optimization Report (PDF)"** — This is currently a free CTA at the bottom. This should be premium. Move it behind the subscription wall.

#### **What to Add**

* **Score projection** — *"If you complete all Quick Wins this week, your Exit Score will move from 62 → 67\. Your valuation will increase by approximately £35,000."* Make the feedback loop immediate and quantified.  
* **AI advisor chat widget** — *"Have a question about how to implement this? Ask your Exit Advisor →"* (Premium only). This is a massive retention driver — human/AI hybrid support makes the subscription feel like it has ongoing value, not just a one-time report.  
* **"Share with your team / VA"** — Many founders will need to delegate these tasks. A shareable action plan (with selective visibility) is a practical premium feature.  
* **Milestone celebrations** — When a user completes a Quick Win action, show a micro-celebration and the score update: *"Score updated: 62 → 63\. You've unlocked £8,000 in additional value."* Gamification of exit preparation.  
* **"Unlock Buyer Matching"** — At the bottom of the Optimization Plan, once a user hits a certain score threshold (e.g. 70+), show: *"You're approaching buyer-ready status. At 70+, we can introduce you to verified buyers in our network. Keep going."* This is the most powerful long-term conversion hook.

#### **Free vs. Premium Gating**

**FREE:** See the top 1 High Priority action (title \+ one-line description, no action steps)

 **GATE:** All action steps, Medium priority actions, the full Implementation Roadmap, the PDF report, AI advisor chat, score projection, buyer matching unlock

## **SECTION 3 — PREMIUM CONVERSION STRATEGY**

### **The Free Tier — What They Get**

The goal of the free tier is ONE thing: the aha moment. They should feel the pain of seeing value left on the table, then immediately see that the solution requires a subscription.

**Free tier includes:**

* Exit Score (overall number only)  
* Top 3 buyer concerns (titles only, no impact values)  
* Valuation range (broad, e.g. £180k–£260k)  
* Top 1 risk (title \+ one-line description)  
* Top 1 optimization action (title only)  
* Business Profile  
* Data Sources (connect up to 2\)

**Everything else is Pro.**

### **The Pro Tier — What They Get (£499/mo)**

* Full 9-dimension breakdown with narratives  
* Full Risk Scanner (all 6 risks \+ buyer psychology)  
* Full Valuation Engine (all drivers, methodology, comparables)  
* Full Optimisation Plan (all actions \+ action steps)  
* AI Exit Advisor chat  
* Saved Reports & PDF downloads  
* Data Source connections (unlimited)  
* Score tracking over time  
* Buyer Matching access (Reagrdless of Score)  
* Expert consultation booking (1 session/month)

### **Blur/Gate Mechanics — How to Execute**

The most effective gating is contextual, not a wall. Here's the hierarchy:

1. **Blurred rows with lock icon** — For dimension scores 4–9 on the Exit Score page. Show the dimension name and a blurred score bar. On hover: *"Unlock this dimension — Pro only."*  
2. **"X more risks identified"** — On Risk Scanner, show a locked card: *"3 more risks are suppressing your value by an estimated £45,000. \[Unlock →\]"*  
3. **Teaser numbers** — On Valuation Engine, show the Optimised scenario (£340k) but blur the "how to get there" section.  
4. **Action step lock** — On Optimization Plan, show the action title, show 1 locked action step (blurred text visible behind a frosted overlay), then a CTA.  
5. **"Buyer View" locked** — A premium feature that shows how your business looks from a buyer's perspective. Teased on dashboard, locked behind Pro.

### **The "Aha Moment" Trigger Points**

The aha moment happens when a user realises the gap between where they are and where they could be — and sees that ExitEcom is the bridge. Design for these specific moments:

1. **The £80,000 card on the dashboard** — *"This is already working. But 73% of users who saw this number couldn't tell you how to close it without Pro."*  
2. **The 3/10 Founder Dependency score** — *"A buyer seeing this will offer you 0.3x less. That's £66,000 gone before negotiation starts."*  
3. **The Optimised scenario (£340k)** — *"This is achievable. But only if you know the exact steps. Unlock the plan."*  
4. **The "12 buyers are looking for businesses like yours"** (dashboard premium card) — This creates FOMO and makes the marketplace feel real and active.

## **SECTION 4 — THE BUYER MATCHING LAYER (PREMIUM HOOK)**

This is your most powerful differentiator and should be woven throughout the experience, not just on one page.

### **Where to Surface Buyer Matching**

**Dashboard:** A persistent card: *"X buyers are actively looking for businesses in \[Beauty & Skincare, £200k–£400k range\]. You'll be introduced when your score reaches 70."*  
 **Exit Readiness Score page:** *"At your current score of 62, you're 8 points from buyer-matching eligibility. Here's what to improve."*  
 **Optimization Plan:** *"Complete these 3 actions and you'll qualify for buyer matching within 4 weeks."*  
 **Settings/Billing:** *"Pro members get matched with buyers. Success tier gets curated introductions \+ M\&A advisor support."*

### **The Expert Consultation Angle**

Rather than just buyer matching, position the human layer as "Exit Advisor" access. This:

* Justifies the £499/mo price psychologically (you're not just paying for software)  
* Creates a pathway to the Success tier (where ExitEcom earns a % of the deal)  
* Builds trust that automated analysis alone can't build

On the Dashboard, add: **"Book a 30-min Exit Strategy Session"** — framed as a premium benefit, not a sales call. *"Review your Exit Score with an expert who's seen 200+ e-commerce exits."*
